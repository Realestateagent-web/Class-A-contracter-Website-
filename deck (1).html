01 — What's Included

No surprises. Everything spelled out.

Decks are code-regulated in Virginia — footings, ledger attachment, and railing height all require inspections. Here's what's in a typical scope.

Included

  • Site layout, permit filing, footing inspections
  • Helical piers or concrete footings (code-depth in NoVA)
  • Pressure-treated structural framing (beams, joists, ledger)
  • Deck boards in your chosen material (PT, cedar, composite)
  • Railings (wood, composite, or aluminum) to code height
  • Stairs and landings with proper rise/run
  • Flashing at ledger board (prevents #1 cause of deck failure)
  • Final inspections and 2-year workmanship warranty

Priced Separately

  • Pergolas, built-in benches, and planters (priced as add-ons)
  • Under-deck drainage systems (Trex RainEscape or similar)
  • Electrical: outlets, post lights, overhead fans (sub-scoped)
  • Hot tubs or spas (we build reinforced pad; you handle the tub)
  • Outdoor kitchens or built-in grills
  • Screened enclosure (easily added later; separate scope)

05 — FAQ

Questions we get every week.

If yours isn't here, scroll back up and ask our AI assistant — or just call and we'll talk it through.

Ask our AI
Pressure-treated, cedar, or composite?

PT is cheapest but requires annual sealing. Cedar looks gorgeous and lasts longer but costs 2–3x PT. Composite (Trex, TimberTech) costs more upfront but requires almost zero maintenance and lasts 25–30 years. Most NoVA clients choose composite these days for the maintenance-free factor.

Do I need a permit for a deck?

In every Northern Virginia county: yes, for anything attached to the house or higher than 30 inches. We handle the whole permit process — you just sign the application.

What size deck is right for our house?

Rule of thumb: the deck shouldn't exceed 20% of the home's main floor footprint, or it looks oversized. For dining + lounging, 300–400 sqft is the sweet spot for most families. We'll help you right-size during the measure visit.

Can we build over our existing deck?

Almost never — existing footings usually don't meet current code, and ledger attachment on old decks is the #1 failure point. We strongly recommend full tear-down and rebuild. The cost difference is smaller than you'd expect.

Will my HOA let me build this?

Most Northern Virginia HOAs require ARB approval, especially for composite material and railing color. We prep the submission package for you. Allow 4–6 weeks for approval before scheduling build.

Can we add a pergola or screened enclosure later?

Yes — we frame the deck now so it can support a pergola or screen enclosure addition later. If there's any chance you'll add either, tell us now so we can oversize the footings appropriately.

Ready to build a deck?

Share photos of your backyard and what you're imagining. We'll come out for a free on-site measure and send a written quote.
