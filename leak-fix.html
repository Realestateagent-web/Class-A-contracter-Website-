05 — FAQ

Questions we get every week.

Ask our AI
Can you come out today?

If it's an active flood, call 703-485-6378 right now. We'll walk you through shutting off the water, triage by phone, and dispatch as fast as our schedule allows — usually same-day or next-morning for genuine emergencies.

How do you find a leak without tearing open my whole wall?

We use infrared cameras, moisture meters, and acoustic leak detectors. 9 times out of 10, we locate within a foot and cut an access hole the size of a notebook. Exploratory demo is a last resort.

Will my insurance cover this?

Sudden leaks (burst pipes, failed water heaters, appliance floods) are usually covered. Slow leaks from poor maintenance often aren't. We document everything with photos and detailed invoices so your adjuster can process the claim cleanly.

What about mold?

Small spots we can spot-treat ($200–500). Anything larger than ~10 sqft or behind walls requires certified mold remediation — we'll refer you to a specialist we trust and coordinate the repair work after.

How do I know if the water is done damaging things?

We use pin-type moisture meters to measure moisture in drywall and framing. Normal readings are under 16% — we dry with fans/dehumidifiers until we hit that before closing walls back up. Rushing this step is how you end up with mold.

Why was my last plumber unable to find the leak?

Leak detection is a specialty skill — not every plumber has it. Rooftop leaks travel along rafters and emerge far from the actual source; plumbing leaks can travel through cavities. We invest in thermal and acoustic tools specifically for hard-to-find leaks.
